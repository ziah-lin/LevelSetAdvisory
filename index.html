The practice

Engineering rigor,
pointed at your numbers.

Level Set Advisory runs on one idea: most teams don't have a strategy problem, they have a baseline problem. They're moving fast on numbers nobody fully trusts, and the gap between the plan and the reality widens quietly.

I'm a mechanical engineer who works in the operations and strategy lane. That means I treat a business problem the way I'd treat a system — map it, isolate the constraint that actually moves the outcome, and build a fix that holds. Scoped tight, delivered in weeks, measured before and after.

The background is real work: integrated financial models across multi-entity structures, stage-gate process design that cut a development cycle, and market modeling for a healthcare imaging portfolio. The throughline is the same — numbers over narrative.
